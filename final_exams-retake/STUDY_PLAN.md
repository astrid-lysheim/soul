## Target: Examen Extraordinario (~June/July 2026)

### What Colosi Actually Tests (from his exams)
NOT plug-and-chug. He wants you to:
1. **Analyze** an ODE qualitatively (monotonicity, max/min, inflection)
2. **Prove** things using theorems (Lagrange, Rolle, contradiction)
3. **Solve** Cauchy problems using THREE methods:
   - Taylor series expansion
   - Picard successive approximations
   - Integrating factors / exact equations
4. **Show** that solution families have certain properties (uniqueness, etc.)

### The Honest Assessment
You need to relearn some prereqs before DiffEq makes sense. No shame ‚Äî this is the plan.

## Phase 1 ‚Äî Core DiffEq (Weeks 4-8)
**Goal:** Learn to solve differential equations

- **Source:** Schaum's Ch. 1-2 + Kaabar "Friendly Introduction" Ch. 1
- Topics: classification (order, linearity), direction fields, initial value problems
- What a "solution" means, solution families, constants of integration

### Week 5: First-Order ODEs ‚Äî Separable & Linear
- **Source:** Schaum's Ch. 3-4 + Boyce & DiPrima Ch. 2
- Topics: separable equations, linear first-order, integrating factors
- Practice: Colosi Exam 2 problem 2 (finding integrating factors)

### Week 6: First-Order ODEs ‚Äî Exact Equations
- **Source:** Schaum's Ch. 5 + Boyce & DiPrima Ch. 2.6
- Topics: exact equations, test for exactness (My = Nx), integrating factors to MAKE exact
- Practice: Colosi Exam 2 problem 1c (full exact equation solution)

### Week 7: Series Solutions & Picard
- **Source:** Paul's Notes (Series Solutions) + Boyce & DiPrima Ch. 5
- Topics: Taylor series solutions, Picard successive approximations
- Practice: Colosi Exam 2 problem 1a and 1b

### Week 8: Qualitative Analysis
- **Source:** Colosi's lecture notes (ENES folder) + Boyce & DiPrima Ch. 2.5
- Topics: autonomous equations, phase lines, stability, monotonicity from the ODE
- Practice: Colosi Exam 1 problem 1 (bacteria population analysis)
