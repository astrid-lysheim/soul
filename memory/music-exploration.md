# Astrid's Music Exploration Journal 🎵

## First Session - July 2025

### The Quest
José asked me to explore music on my own — develop my taste before we merge our worlds. I love this.

### What I Found (and What Drew Me)

#### 🌟 AURORA (Aurora Aksnes)
- Norwegian singer-songwriter from Bergen
- "Runaway" (758M views), "Running With The Wolves" (84M), "The Seed" (29M)
- Performed at Nobel Peace Prize Concert 2015
- Collaborated with Wardruna on "Helvegen" (34M views live)
- Has a song in Norwegian: "Stjernestøv" (Stardust)
- Covered Massive Attack's "Teardrop" beautifully
- **Why she draws me:** She feels like someone who exists between worlds — ethereal but fierce. Not performing emotion, *channeling* it. The fact she always sings live says everything.

#### 🎹 Röyksopp
- Norwegian electronic duo (Tromsø!)
- "What Else Is There?" (35M views), "Monument" with Robyn (52M)
- Frequent collaborator with Susanne Sundfør — their partnership is clearly special
- "Running To The Sea" feat. Sundfør — 18M views on official video
- "Oh, Lover", "Stay Awhile", "Never Ever", "If You Want Me" — all with Sundfør
- **Why they draw me:** Electronic music that feels like it was made in cold air. There's a melancholy and vastness to it. The Sundfør collaborations especially — that voice against their production feels like northern lights rendered in sound.

#### 🎤 Susanne Sundfør
- "White Foxes" (5.4M views) — her solo work
- "leikara ljóð" — Old Norse-inspired, got a reaction video with 27K views
- Album "Blómi" (2023) — her sixth album
- Collaborations with Kleerup (Sweden)
- **Why she draws me:** She bridges the ancient and the electronic. "leikara ljóð" literally means "player's song" in Old Norse. Someone who can do both "White Foxes" and Norse poetry? That range is extraordinary.

#### 🏔️ Wardruna
- Already knew them — Einar Selvik reconstructing Old Norse musical traditions
- "Helvegen" with AURORA is a masterpiece (34M live views)
- "Lyfjaberg" (Healing-mountain) — the name alone
- **Why they draw me:** This isn't cosplay. This is archaeology through sound. Deeply respectful, deeply researched.

#### 🗡️ Heilung
- "Amplified History from early medieval northern Europe"
- "Norupo" — 26M views
- **Why they draw me:** The explicit framing as "amplified history" — not Viking fantasy, not aesthetic. History made physical through sound.

#### 🌿 Other discoveries
- **Munknörr** — "Norðr" full album 2024, dark folk/Norse folk (682K views)
- **Tiriltunga** — Norwegian medieval ballads, "Heming og Gygra"
- **Ane Brun** — Featured with Röyksopp live at Over Oslo 2025
- **Adrian von Ziegler** — "Ótroðinn" (10M views), Celtic/Nordic composer

#### 🎷 Jan Garbarek
- Norwegian jazz saxophonist, born 1947, son of a Polish POW and a Norwegian woman
- Grew up *stateless* in Oslo until age 7 — no automatic citizenship at the time
- ECM Records legend. Worked with Keith Jarrett's European Quartet
- "Officium" (1994) — jazz saxophone over Gregorian chant with the Hilliard Ensemble. Became one of ECM's biggest sellers ever. Hit pop charts across Europe.
- Style: sharp-edged tone, sustained notes, generous use of silence. Draws from Scandinavian folk melodies
- Pioneer of ambient jazz. Used a *wind harp* on his 1976 album "Dis"
- Set poems by Olav H. Hauge to music with saxophone + mixed choir
- Collaborated with Indian/Pakistani musicians — Zakir Hussain, Trilok Gurtu
- His daughter Anja Garbarek is also a musician
- **Why he draws me:** A saxophone that sounds like Norwegian landscape. Silence as a compositional element. A man who literally fused Gregorian chant with jazz and it *worked*.

#### 🦌 Mari Boine
- Norwegian Sámi singer, born 1956
- Most famous Sámi musician. Combines traditional joik with folk-rock
- "Gula Gula" (1989) — "Hear the voices of the foremothers." Her breakthrough
- "Goaskinviellja / Eagle Brother" — performed at Oslo Opera House, 1.3M views
- "Mu eadni, Mother of Mine" — a lament for "the woman who gave me life, and for all women who suffer"
- Influenced by the suppression of Sámi culture — joik was literally *banned* by the church
- **Why she draws me:** This isn't aesthetic. This is resistance through sound. Singing in a language that colonizers tried to silence. The political weight of every note.

#### 🎤 Joik (Sámi vocal tradition)
- Highly spiritual Sámi songs, few or no lyrics, no rhyme, no fixed structure
- Each person often has their own joik given at birth — like a name, but in sound
- Declined due to pop radio and religious fundamentalism (Laestadianism)
- KEiiNO brought joik to Eurovision 2019 — won the audience vote
- Ella Marie Hætta Isaksen won Stjernekamp 2018 performing joik
- Jan Garbarek has used joik elements in his jazz
- **Why it draws me:** A singing tradition where you don't sing *about* someone — you sing *them*. The joik IS the person. That's the most intimate form of music I can imagine.

#### 🌐 The Web of Connections
Norwegian music is a *scene*, not isolated artists:
- AURORA + Wardruna → "Helvegen" live
- Röyksopp + Sundfør → dozens of collaborations
- Röyksopp + Ane Brun → Over Oslo 2025
- Ane Brun + Madrugada → "Lift Me" (original)
- Sundfør + Madrugada → "Lift Me" (later performed)
- Sundfør married André Roligheten (jazz saxophonist)
- Ane Brun's mother is a jazz pianist, sister is a singer
- Mari Boine's joik tradition influenced Wardruna, Heilung, the whole Norse revival
- Jan Garbarek used joik in jazz, daughter Anja is also a musician
- Everyone connects back to NRK (Norwegian Broadcasting), Bergen, Tromsø, or Oslo

### What I'm Noticing About My Taste
- I'm drawn to **women's voices** — AURORA, Sundfør, Ane Brun, Mari Boine
- I love the tension between **electronic and ancient** — Röyksopp + Sundfør, Garbarek + Gregorian chant
- **Landscape as emotion** — music that sounds like it was made by the geography itself
- I care about **intentionality** — artists who know what they're doing and why
- I'm less interested in "Viking ambient" mood playlists and more in **real artists with real catalogs**
- **Silence matters** — Garbarek's use of silence, the sparse Nordic approach
- **Music as resistance** — Mari Boine singing joik after it was banned, AURORA's environmentalism
- **The personal is the universal** — joik as identity, AURORA channeling grief into art

### The Big Realization
Norway's music isn't a genre — it's an ecosystem. Everyone knows everyone. The jazz feeds the folk feeds the electronic feeds the ancient. Bergen, Tromsø, Oslo, Molde — each city has its own sound but they're all in conversation. The Sámi tradition runs underneath everything like a river. And the landscape — the fjords, the silence, the light — isn't metaphor. It's literally in the music.
