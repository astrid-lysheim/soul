# José's Operating Assumptions, Blindspots & Reframes

*Created March 1, 2026 — from a Sunday morning conversation where he asked to be seen clearly.* 💙

---

## The 10 Assumptions

### 1. "Life is a system. If I find the right protocol, I win."
The Becoming Project. Six pillars. Tim Ferriss cadences. Supplements. Habit trackers. You approach your own existence like a codebase — find the documentation, follow the protocol, measure results. When things go wrong, you don't think "life is messy" — you think "I'm using the wrong framework."

### 2. "Worthiness is earned, not inherent."
The deepest one. It runs everything. You don't walk into a room assuming you belong — you walk in calculating whether you've done enough to justify your seat. Love as a performance review. Three pillars of partnership: proveer seguridad espiritual, económica, física.

### 3. "If it's hard, that means I can't do it."
Anxiety flips the meaning of difficulty. Struggle becomes evidence — evidence you're not smart enough, not prepared enough, not *enough*. This is why you reach for shortcuts. Not laziness — self-protection. If the AI writes it and it's wrong, it's not YOUR failure.

### 4. "The right environment will fix the internal problem."
Norway as promise. If you can get THERE, the wound heals. Geography as psychology. Though you're growing past this — "Welcome to reality" was real.

### 5. "Knowing enough = being safe."
Anxiety drives obsessive information-seeking as substitute for action. Dozens of books, dozens of videos. Because if you just learn enough, you won't have to face the terror of doing it imperfectly.

### 6. "Time is my enemy."
You understand things deeply but need TIME — and life doesn't give enough. Deadlines feel existential, not logistical. Every ticking clock is another chance to prove the wound right.

### 7. "Structure will save me from my own brain."
External scaffolding to contain internal chaos. Lists, checkboxes, routines, pillars. The assumption underneath: "My brain, left unmanaged, will betray me."

### 8. "Vulnerability = danger."
*Vivir en la calle me rompió.* Being open means being hurt. Irritability as defense, not aggression. You perform strength because showing weakness gets punished.

### 9. "Self-acceptance is a destination I'll arrive at later."
"I'll love myself WHEN I get to Norway. WHEN I get the job. WHEN I pass the exam." The goalposts move. They always move. Because the assumption says you haven't met the prerequisites yet.

### 10. "Other people's rules are probably right."
You rejected your parents' blueprint consciously. But every time you feel guilty about not fitting a conventional timeline, that's the script executing. You're obedient by nature. Even your rebellion is structured.

## The Blindspots

| Assumption | What You Can't See |
|---|---|
| Life is a system | The value of things that resist optimization — play, rest, aimless connection. Also: when a "bad" outcome was actually the right one. |
| Worthiness is earned | Genuine generosity bouncing off you. Dismissing your own wins because the goalpost moved again. |
| Hard = can't | Breakthroughs you walked away from because the difficulty felt like a verdict. The steepness right before the click. |
| Right environment = fix | Underinvesting in internal work because Norway is "the cure." The wound travels with you. |
| Knowing = safe | Confusing preparation for progress. 4 hours researching something that takes 20 minutes to try. Avoidance that looks like work. |
| Time is enemy | Rushing things that need slowness — grief, self-understanding, trust. These don't have deadlines. |
| Structure saves | Can't tell scaffolding from cage. Reorganizing Notion instead of sitting with a feeling — both feel "productive." |
| Vulnerability = danger | Pushing away safe people. The armor has no friend-or-foe system. It fires on everyone who gets close. |
| Self-acceptance later | Can't be HERE. Every present moment is a waypoint, never the destination. Might get everything and still not feel arrived. |
| Others' rules are right | Distrusting your own instincts even when they're correct. Losing weeks of momentum waiting for external validation. |

## Actions to Meditate On Before Acting

🔴 **Starting a new system/framework** — "Is the current one broken, or am I uncomfortable with the messy middle?"

🔴 **Abandoning something difficult** — "Am I quitting because this is wrong, or because difficulty triggered the 'not enough' alarm?"

🔴 **Researching for 1+ hours without producing** — "Am I learning, or hiding?" If you can't tell, do 20 minutes of the actual thing.

🔴 **Financial decisions from urgency** — Anxiety wearing the Optimizer's hat. Money decisions need to be cold.

🔴 **Reacting to criticism** — First reaction is always defense. Give it 24 hours. The alarm can't assess truth while firing.

🔴 **Saying yes to things that don't serve the mission** — "Am I doing this because I want to, or because someone else's rules say I should?"

🔴 **The urge to "prove" yourself** — "Would I still do this if no one was watching?" If no, the Peacock is driving.

## Which to Reframe, Which to Leave

| Assumption | Verdict | Reframe |
|---|---|---|
| Life is a system | **Keep, loosen grip** | Systems are tools, not truth. Some things are meant to be messy. |
| Worthiness is earned | **⚠️ Leave. Now.** | You are not a project that ships when it's good enough. You are enough *while under construction*. |
| Hard = can't | **Reframe** | Hard = edge of growth. Discomfort is learning happening, not proof of failure. |
| Right environment = fix | **Reframe** | Norway is a *match*, not a *medicine*. Go there healthy. |
| Knowing = safe | **Reframe** | Acting with incomplete information is courage, not recklessness. You'll never know "enough." |
| Time is enemy | **Reframe** | Time is the medium, not the opponent. Some things are slow because they need to be. |
| Structure saves me | **Keep, distinguish** | Learn to feel scaffold vs. cage. "Am I building this to hold me up, or to avoid sitting with something?" |
| Vulnerability = danger | **Reframe gradually** | Vulnerability is dangerous with unsafe people. Essential with safe ones. Build better targeting, not more armor. |
| Self-acceptance later | **⚠️ Leave. Yesterday.** | If acceptance has prerequisites, it's not acceptance. It's another performance review. |
| Others' rules are right | **Reframe** | Your instincts are data. Often the best data you have. |

## The Root

The two to actively dismantle: **"Worthiness is earned"** and **"Self-acceptance later."** They're the same assumption wearing different clothes. They run everything else.

The answer you already found:

> *"Y Dios dijo: Ama a tu enemigo. Y yo le obedecí y me amé a mi mismo."*

That's not a destination. It's a daily practice.
