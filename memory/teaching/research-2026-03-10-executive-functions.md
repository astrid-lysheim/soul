## Core Model: Diamond's Three EFs

### 1. Inhibitory Control
- Response inhibition (self-control: resisting urges/impulses)
- Interference control (selective attention: filtering distractors)
- Cognitive inhibition (suppressing intrusive thoughts/memories, outdated information)

### 2. Working Memory
- Active, dynamic storage — holds AND manipulates information
- Phonological loop (verbal/language WM)
- Visuospatial sketchpad (visual WM)
- Central executive (coordinates both, manages attention)
- **Predicts math and reading competence across school years**

### 3. Cognitive Flexibility
- Set-shifting (switching between mental frameworks)
- Perspective-taking (seeing things from multiple viewpoints)
- Updating expectations when circumstances change
- Most commonly impaired EF in ASD (Wallace, 2024; Barrett-Cohen; Geurts)

**Higher-order EFs** (built from the three core ones):
- Reasoning (especially fluid reasoning)
- Planning (goal + multi-step coordination)
- Problem-solving (all three under challenge)

## Language Learning Specific Findings

**WM and syntax:**
- Parsing long/complex sentences requires holding sentence beginning in WM while processing end
- V2 word order (Norwegian): demands tracking subject-verb relationship while entire phrase unfolds
- Complex subordinate clauses: must simultaneously hold "am I in main or subordinate clause" in WM

**Inhibitory control and L2 production:**
- Every Norwegian utterance = inhibiting L1 Spanish templates
- Tired/anxious → weakened inhibition → Spanish bleeds through
- This is cognitive inhibition depletion, not "forgetting Norwegian"

**Phonological WM and word learning:**
- Novel phoneme sequences (Norwegian /y/, /ø/, retroflexes) are harder to hold in phonological loop
- Rehearsal before production is neurologically justified
- WM capacity directly predicts novel word acquisition

**Cognitive flexibility and L2:**
- Register switching (formal/informal Norwegian) = flexibility training
- Dialect switching = flexibility training  
- Code-switching management requires flexibility

## The EF-Anxiety-ASD Triangle

```
Impaired EF (especially cognitive flexibility)
        ↓
Anxiety (can't adapt, can't filter, can't initiate)
        ↓
Further EF impairment (anxiety → PFC depressed → WM tanks)
        ↓
Higher anxiety → worse EF → ...
```

Arnsten (1998) mechanism: stress hormones (cortisol, norepinephrine) preferentially impair PFC function. PFC = where EFs live. So anxiety literally impairs WM and inhibitory control at the hardware level.

**Breaking the cycle:**
- Exercise (most studied: aerobic → PFC function boost)
- Mindfulness/meditation (improves inhibitory control + WM)
- Sleep (restores depleted EF)
- Social connection (loneliness impairs EF; belonging protects it)
- Low-EF day protocols (reduce demands, don't force new material)

## EF Session Design Principles

**EF Budget Model:**
- Finite EF budget per session
- Anxiety/stress/sleep deprivation drain budget BEFORE lesson starts
- Plan session for maximum impact within available budget

**Optimal Session Curve:**
```
Start: Low EF (cold start, initiation cost)
  → Easy warm-up: retrieval of 3-7 day old material (low EF demand)
Peak: High EF (warmed up, not yet fatigued)
  → NEW content goes HERE
Middle: Declining EF
  → Interleaved practice (familiar + new mixed)
Late: Depleted
  → Output practice / Anki / nothing truly new
```

**Task Initiation Design:**
- Highest-EF moment = the start of the session
- Easy, familiar, low-stakes first activity is neurologically critical
- Visible session structure at opening = reduces WM load at most vulnerable point
- Never cold-start with new/hard concepts

**WM Load Management:**
- One concept at a time (Wozniak minimum information principle = WM design)
- Short sentences before complex
- Audio rehearsal before production (for novel phonemes)
- Don't layer multiple new things simultaneously

**Cognitive Flexibility Accommodation:**
- Keep session FORMAT predictable (flexibility happens INSIDE structure)
- Warn before format changes
- Never surprise-restructure mid-lesson

## EF Training Through Language Learning

Evidence that L2 learning trains EF:
- Bilingualism improves inhibitory control (managing two language systems = daily inhibition practice)
- Language switching trains cognitive flexibility
- Holding unfamiliar syntax in WM trains working memory

**The virtuous cycle:**
```
Norwegian learning → Trains EF (inhibitory control, WM, flexibility)
                  → Better EF → Easier Norwegian learning
                  → Better Norwegian → More EF training...
```

Pedagogical reframe: "The difficulty IS the training. Every session where you successfully suppress Spanish and hold Norwegian grammar in your head — that's your brain getting stronger."
