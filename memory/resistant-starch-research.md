## José's Protocol

**Product:** Bob's Red Mill Unmodified Potato Starch (~8g RS per tablespoon)
**Cost:** ~650 MXN per 4 bags (680g each) = ~162 MXN/bag
**Monthly need:** ~2.5 bags/month at target dose = ~405 MXN/month
**Target dose:** 40g RS/day (5 tablespoons) — ramp up over 3-4 weeks
**Duration:** Minimum 4 months to match study protocols
**Total cost estimate:** ~1,620 MXN for full 4-month protocol

### Ramp-Up Schedule
- Week 1: 1 tbsp/day (~8g)
- Week 2: 2 tbsp/day (~16g)
- Week 3: 3-4 tbsp/day (~24-32g)
- Week 4+: 5 tbsp/day (~40g)

### How to Take
- Mix in COLD water, smoothie, or yogurt
- Do NOT heat — cooking destroys resistant starch
- Take 10-15 min before meals (per study protocol)

## Studies (Downloaded)

### 1. Ni et al. 2023 — Cell Metabolism (The Big NAFLD Trial)
**File:** `~/Downloads/Cell Press_20260315/Resistant-starch-decreases-intrahepatic-triglyceri.pdf`
- **Design:** RCT, placebo-controlled, 200 patients with NAFLD
- **Dose:** 40g HAM-RS2 (hi-maize corn) daily for **4 months**
- **Primary outcome:** Intrahepatic triglyceride content (IHTC) reduced from 25% → 13% (HALVED)
- **Secondary:** BMI down, VFA down, SFA down, liver enzymes improved
- **Weight loss:** −5.5 kg in treatment group
- **NAFLD response rate:** 47% (≥30% reduction = histological improvement)
- **Mechanism:** Gut microbiome alteration — reduced *Bacteroides stercoris* (steatosis-promoting)
- **Key finding:** Effect was INDEPENDENT of weight loss — RS disproportionately targets liver/visceral fat
- **Note:** Patients received dietary guidelines only, not formulated diets (potential confounder)

### 2. Li et al. 2024 — Nature Metabolism (Overweight/Obese)
**File:** `~/Downloads/42255_2024_Article_988.pdf`
- **Design:** RCT, double-blind, crossover, 37 participants with overweight/obesity
- **Dose:** 40g HAM-RS2 daily for **8 weeks** (2 × 8-week periods with 4-week washout)
- **Results:**
  - Weight loss: −2.8 kg (vs. control)
  - VFA: 108 → 86 cm² (p<0.001)
  - Waist circumference: significantly reduced
  - Insulin resistance: improved (GIR increased)
  - Inflammation: TNFα and IL-1β reduced
  - Fecal lipids: INCREASED → RS blocks fat absorption!
- **Mechanism:** *Bifidobacterium adolescentis* identified as key species. Fecal transplant from RS-treated humans → mice got same benefits. Causal link established.
- **Diet:** Strictly controlled (3 identical meals/day) — strongest design

### 3. Zhang et al. 2019 — Scientific Reports (Normal Weight)
**File:** `~/Downloads/41598_2018_Article_38216.pdf`
- **Design:** RCT, double-blind, crossover, 19 normal-weight participants
- **Dose:** 40g HAM-RS2 daily for **4 weeks**
- **Results:**
  - VFA: significantly reduced (even in normal-weight!)
  - SFA: also reduced
  - LDL cholesterol: decreased
  - GLP-1 (satiety hormone): increased at 30 min post-meal
  - Acetate (SCFA): significantly increased
  - No GI side effects
  - NO significant body weight change — but fat redistribution occurred
- **Microbiome:** *Ruminococcaceae_UCG-005* increased; 15 genera decreased
- **Key insight:** RS works on fat distribution even without weight loss

### 4. Zhang & Yu 2023 — Cell Metabolism Preview/Commentary
**File:** `~/Downloads/Cell Press_20260315/Vive-la-resistant-starch--A-potential-treatment-fo.pdf`
- **Type:** Expert commentary on the Ni et al. 2023 trial (3 pages)
- **Key points:**
  - ≥30% liver fat reduction associated with histological improvement in steatohepatitis
  - Caution: 4 months may be insufficient to establish long-term clinical benefit
  - Inter-individual microbiome variation may affect results
  - Suggests targeting *B. stercoris* specifically (antibiotics + inulin combo showed promise in prior work)
  - Standardized diets recommended for future studies
  - Questions: how long must patients adhere to benefit?

## Key Takeaways

1. **40g/day is the magic number** — all successful studies used this dose; lower doses (1/3 to 1/2) failed
2. **HAM-RS2 from corn** was used in all studies — Bob's potato RS2 has similar mechanism but wasn't directly tested
3. **Works across weight categories** — overweight, obese, normal-weight, NAFLD
4. **4-8 weeks minimum** to see body composition changes; 4 months for full NAFLD reversal
5. **Mechanism is via gut microbiome** — not just calories. RS feeds beneficial bacteria → SCFA production → reduced inflammation → reduced fat absorption → visceral fat loss
6. **Key bacteria:** ↑ *B. adolescentis*, *Ruminococcaceae*; ↓ *B. stercoris*
7. **No significant GI side effects** reported in any study (with controlled ramp-up)
8. **Probiotics not required** — RS itself reshapes the microbiome; but synbiotic approach could theoretically enhance results

## Probiotics Question

José asked whether to take probiotics before starting RS. Based on the studies:
- **Not necessary.** All studies used RS alone, no probiotics, and saw significant microbiome changes.
- RS acts as a PREBIOTIC — it feeds existing beneficial bacteria.
- If adding probiotics, look for *Bifidobacterium adolescentis* strains specifically (the causally linked species).
- Start RS first, consider probiotics as an add-on after 4-6 weeks if desired.
