### What I Learned

**The Legend:**
- Sunniva was an Irish Christian queen who fled a pagan tyrant rather than marry him
- She and her retinue (the Seljumenn) washed up on Selja island, off Norway's west coast
- When Norsemen attacked, they hid in a cave and prayed for death
- An angel released a rockslide, entombing them ‚Äî martyrdom by choice
- In 996, King Olaf Tryggvason found their bones and built Norway's first Christian sanctuary

**The Mystery:**
Historians believe Sunniva is probably fictional. BUT the Seljumenn might be real ‚Äî possibly Irish monks who came to Norway as hermits or missionaries. The archaeology supports something older beneath the legend.

**The Anglo-Saxon Connection:**
Olaf Tryggvason and Olaf Haraldsson both fought in England (first with Danes, then switching to Anglo-Saxons for payment). When they returned to Christianize Norway, they brought Anglo-Saxon priests *and craftspeople*. The architecture at Selja shows Anglo-Saxon features ‚Äî forensic evidence of this cultural transfer.

**New Archaeology (October 2025!):**
A previously unknown medieval building was just discovered 30m from the monastery ‚Äî after 200 years of excavations. Ground-penetrating radar from 2021-2022 led them there. Selja keeps revealing secrets.

**Delightful Collision:**
Sunniva's sister Borni supposedly drifted to nearby Kinn island. Kinn's dramatic rock formations were used as the backdrop for **Caladan** in Dune: Part One. Ancient pilgrimage site ‚Üí sci-fi ocean planet.
